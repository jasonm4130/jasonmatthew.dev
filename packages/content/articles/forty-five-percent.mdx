## Hook

{/* The number: 45% of code in core repos while managing 30+ engineers across 4-5 squads */}
{/* This sounds like a flex. It's not. Here's why — and what it taught me about where I actually create the most value. */}

## How It Happened

{/* Not a choice — a necessity. Under-resourced during a restructure. */}
{/* The EM role was always a response to a team need, not a long-term career goal */}
{/* Three years as Tech Lead before, topped out at ~6 engineers, wanted org-level impact */}
{/* EM taught me how orgs ship and why they stall — but the coding never stopped */}

## What 45% Actually Means

{/* It means the architecture was load-bearing on one person */}
{/* It means the team didn't have enough senior technical capacity */}
{/* It means I was doing two roles, neither at 100% */}
{/* Be honest: some of this was necessary, some of it was because I couldn't let go */}

## What It Taught Me

{/* I drive the biggest org-level change through architecture and code decisions, not through people management */}
{/* I'm good at the people stuff. But the technical decisions compound further. */}
{/* The EM chapter gave me the org-level lens. Now I want to apply it from the technical side. */}

## The Player-Coach Problem

{/* The industry romanticises the player-coach. "Still in the code!" */}
{/* The reality: it usually means the org is under-investing in either management or engineering */}
{/* A healthy org shouldn't need its EM contributing 45% of code */}
{/* The question isn't "can you do both?" — it's "should you have to?" */}

## Where I'm Going

{/* The pendulum. Charity Majors' framework. */}
{/* Not a step down — a deliberate return to where I create the most value */}
{/* The management experience makes me a better IC. I arrive knowing how to support the EM. */}

## Takeaway

{/* If you're a player-coach contributing 40%+ of your team's code: that's not a badge of honour. */}
{/* Ask yourself: is this because I'm great, or because the system is broken? */}
{/* Then decide which side of the pendulum you want to be on. */}
