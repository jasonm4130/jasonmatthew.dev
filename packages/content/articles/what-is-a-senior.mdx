This is a question I get asked a lot — by the engineers I manage, by candidates in interviews, and honestly by myself when I reflect on my own growth.

The answer isn't about years. I've seen engineers with 3 years of experience who operate at a senior level, and engineers with 15 years who don't.

## It's About Impact Radius

The biggest shift from mid to senior is the radius of your impact. A mid-level engineer solves the problem in front of them. A senior engineer solves the class of problems, and ideally makes it so others don't hit the same problem again.

## It's About Judgment

Senior engineers make good trade-offs. They know when to take shortcuts and when to invest in the right abstraction. They can hold the tension between "ship it" and "do it right" and make the call based on context, not dogma.

## It's About Communication

The best senior engineers I've worked with are excellent communicators. They can explain complex technical decisions to non-technical stakeholders. They write clear RFCs. They give feedback that makes people better.

## It's About Ownership

Senior engineers own outcomes, not just outputs. They don't just write the code — they make sure the feature actually solves the user's problem, the deployment goes smoothly, and the on-call team knows what changed.
